Synthetic biology

15 January 2017

Biology and computer science what? Yes, Synthetic Biology is an interdisciplinary branch of biology and engineering. The subject combines various disciplines from within these domains, such as biotechnology, evolutionary biology, molecular biology, systems biology, biophysics, computer engineering and genetic engineering.

Experts predict that within ten years, it will be possible to search for genetic designs on the web, download them to your computer and modify and adapt these to your needs. Cold and flu vaccine designs will be spread quickly over social media and that the process will be as easy as downloading an app on a smartphone. This technology will, ideally, make it possible for us to print our own treatments.

But it also means the possibility of terrorists creating biotoxins or virus strains that could be unleashed over large populations. They will also be able to create targeted attacks against a single individual based on his or her own unique biology.

CRISPR, a new genome editing tool, could transform the field of biology. It has the potential to correct genetic mutations and cure life-threatening diseases. While gene editing gives us a way to alter genes that cause diseases there is a huge possibility of its misuse.

With internet of things expected to compromise thirty billion connected devices by 2020, one of the most exciting areas of focus is now on nanosensors capable of circulating in the human body. Once connected, this Internet of Nano things could have a huge impact on future of medicine, agriculture and drug manufacture.

With these emerging technologies, bioethicists have begun debating the ethics of this technology. Biological systems are extremely complex and changing human genes could have unintended and undesirable consequences. And many bioethicists are not comfortable with the prospect of gene editing for purely cosmetic enhancements, or to give certain people physical and intellectual advantages.

However, it is too late to turn the clock back on these emerging technologies. The genie ( or should I say the gene) is already out of the bottle.

Exponential technologies are driving changes in business models and changing the way companies operate. How will it impact you? And what can you do about it? That I believe is the important 'So what' question. So what?
